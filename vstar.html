VSTAR

I worked with a team to redesign the online version of Purdue University's student orientation program.

We evaluated the existing product, planned and conducted user research, tested a prototype and delivered a set of design requirements for the product.

  • Heuristic Analysis

    We started with a Heuristic Analysis of the current orientation process. We used an adaptation of Nielsen's heuristics for online applications to find the strengths and weaknesses of the current design using Blackboard.

    From our analysis, we found that most of VSTAR's weaknesses came from Blackboard. We decided to redesign VSTAR from scratch.

  • Data Collection

    We conducted three interviews - one with an academic advisor, two with students.

    One of the students had participated in STAR - Purdue's on-campus orientation program. The other had participated in VSTAR.

    From these interviews - along with an open-ended survey - we learned about our user demographics and their expectations and needs.

  • Analysis

    From our notes and surveys, we used an affinity diagram to develop a persona for our primary user group.

    We used our persona to identify scenarios and design requirements.

  • Ideation

    We sketched several ideas and settled on a design for our first prototype.

  • Usability Testing

    We created a set of paper prototypes and recruited five participants - three high school students, and two college students - to test the usability of our design.

  • Deliverables

    We presented our client with a report on our findings and our prototype.
